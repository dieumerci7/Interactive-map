INTRO
Welcome to the Many Faces of Ukraine, a digital historical mapping platform visualising ethnic diversity of Ukraine’s lands across the twentieth century. The platform allows the user to explore and compare demographic changes in Ukraine, especially among its numerous ethnic communities. It provides a short overview of the ethnic diversity on Ukraine’s lands through available data on ethnic groups, and their native languages in Ukraine in historical perspective.
All data gathered stem from historical censuses organized by imperial, Soviet and Ukrainian governments. In particular, the researchers’ team analyzed the 1897 Russian Imperial Census, 1910 Austrian imperial census, Romanian census of 1930, Czechoslovak census of 1930, Polish census of 1931, as well as Soviet censuses of 1926, 1959, 1970, 1979, 1989. The last census analyzed was conducted in independent Ukraine in 2001.
