Before 1991, the Ukrainian SSR consisted of 25 oblasts and two cities of republican subordination, Kyiv and Sevastopol.
In 1954, the new Cherkasy oblast was created out of the southern raions of Kyiv oblast, and some raions of Poltava and Kirovohrad oblasts. Same year, Izmail oblast was merged into Odesa oblast; and Crimean oblast was transferred from the Rusian RSFSR to Ukrainian SSR.
In 1958, Voroshylovhrad oblast was renamed Luhansk. And in 1959, Drohobych oblast merged with Lviv oblast.
This map reflects the administrative division of Ukraine as of 2015 and should be used only as a reference.
