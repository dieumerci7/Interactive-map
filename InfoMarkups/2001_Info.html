2001
This map shows the percentages of residents of each oblast categorized by ethnicity and native languages on the territory of today’s Ukraine according to the results of the 2001 Ukrainian census.
On 24 August 1991, Ukraine proclaimed its independence. Ukraine consists of 24 oblasts, 2 cities with special status (Kyiv and Sevastopol), and one autonomous republic (Crimea).
According to the first All-Ukraine census, Ukraine’s population was 48,241 thousand persons, 77.8 per cent of which declared “Ukrainian” as their ethnicity.
This map reflects the administrative division of Ukraine as of 2015 and should be used only as a reference.
