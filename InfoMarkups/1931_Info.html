1931
This map shows the percentages of residents for different administrative units categorized by ethnicity (alternatively, by native language) on the territory of today’s Ukraine as of 1931.
For the territories belonging to the Ukrainian Socialist Soviet Republic, the data on the population of each district (raion) by ethnicity (natsional’nost’) is from the 1926 First All- Union Census of the Soviet Union. The map reflects the administrative division as of the March 1, 1927, and is the courtesy of the Harvard Ukrainian Research Institute.
For the territories belonging to the Kingdom of Romania (Bukovina, Hotyn and Southern Bessarabia), the data on the population of each county (județul) by native language is from the 1930 census.
For the territories belonging to the Republic of Poland (Eastern Galicia and Volhynia), the data on the population of each county (powiat) by native language is from the 1931 census.
For the territories belonging to the Czechoslovak Republic (Subcarpathia), the data on the population of each county (okres) by ethnicity is from the 1930 census.
For the territories belonging to the Russian Soviet Federative Socialist Republic (Crimea), the data on the population of each district (raion) by ethnicity (natsional’nost’) is from the 1926 First All-Union Census of the Soviet Union.
